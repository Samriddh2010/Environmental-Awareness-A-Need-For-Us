CAUSES OF ENVIRONMENTAL DEGRADATION

    Environmental degradation is the deterioration of the environment through the depletion of natural resources, destruction of ecosystems, and loss of biodiversity. Major causes of environmental degradation are:-
  • Deforestation: Cutting down forests for wood, agriculture, and urban development reduces biodiversity, disrupts water cycles, and increases carbon dioxide in the atmosphere further resulting in global warming.

  • Pollution: Air, water, and soil pollution from industrial activities, transportation, and improper waste disposal contaminates natural resources and harms living organisms.

  • Overpopulation: Rapid population growth increases the demand for resources, leading to overexploitation and disturb the balance of the environment.

  • Urbanization: Expansion of cities and infrastructure leads to habitat destruction, increased waste generation, and higher energy consumption.

  • Climate Change: Emission of greenhouse gases like methane, carbon dioxide and nitrous oxide from human activities cause global warming, leading to extreme weather events, sea-level rise, and ecosystem disruptions.

EFFECTS OF ENVIRONMENTAL DEGRADATION

  • Ecosystem Imbalance: Disruption of natural processes and food chains can destabilize ecosystems, making them less resilient to changes and external shocks.

  • Soil Degradation: Erosion, deforestation, and industrial activities reduce soil fertility and productivity, affecting plant growth and agriculture.

  • Exposure to Toxins: Chemicals and hazardous waste can cause various health issues, including cancer, birth defects, and neurological disorders.

  • Loss of Livelihoods: Communities that depend on natural resources, such as fishing, farming, and tourism, may suffer economically from degraded environments

  • Displacement and Migration: Environmental degradation can force people to migrate due to loss of homes, livelihoods, or resources, leading to social conflicts and pressure on urban areas.

  • Quality of Life: Reduced access to clean air, water, and natural spaces can negatively impact mental and physical well-being.

  • Global warming is the long-term heating of Earth's surface observed since the pre-industrial period (between 1850 and 1900) due to human activities, primarily fossil fuel burning, which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth's atmosphere
