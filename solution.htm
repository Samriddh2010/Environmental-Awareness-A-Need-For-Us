SOLUTIONS FOR ENVIRONMENTAL PROBLEMS

  • Reduce Carbon Emissions: Shift to renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power. Implement energy efficiency measures in industries, buildings, and transportation.

  • Reforestation and Afforestation: Plant trees and restore forests to absorb CO2 from the atmosphere.

  • Reduce Plastic Waste: Encourage the use of reusable products, implement recycling programs, and develop biodegradable alternatives.

  • Protect Natural Habitats: Establish protected areas and enforce laws against illegal logging.

  • Efficient Water Use: Promote water-saving technologies and practices in agriculture, industry, and households.

  • Promote Sustainable Cities: Develop green infrastructure, increase urban green spaces, and promote public transportation.

  • Improve Urban Planning: Design cities to be more efficient and less resource-intensive, incorporating sustainable practices in construction and energy use.

ACTIONS TAKEN BY INDIAN GOVERNMENT TO ADDRESS ENVIRONMENTAL PROBLEMS

  • National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC): Launched in 2008, NAPCC outlines eight missions focused on climate change mitigation and adaptation, including solar energy, enhanced energy efficiency, sustainable agriculture, and water conservation.

  • Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Clean India Mission): Initiated in 2014, this nationwide campaign aims to improve sanitation and cleanliness, with a focus on eliminating open defecation and managing waste effectively.

  • National Green Tribunal (NGT): Established in 2010, the NGT is a specialized judicial body that handles cases related to environmental protection and conservation of forests and natural resources.

  • Renewable Energy Initiatives: India has set ambitious targets for renewable energy, aiming to achieve 175 GW of renewable energy capacity by 2022, including 100 GW from solar power. This goal has been further increased to 450 GW by 2030.

  • Clean Ganga Mission (Namami Gange): Launched in 2014, this project aims to rejuvenate the Ganga River by implementing pollution abatement activities and improving water quality.
